Our Stem Cell Treatment Technologies

Advanced protocols for maximal quality and safety.

The success of stem-cell-based therapies begins with the adoption of cell processing and manufacturing methods that promote maximal quality and safety of the cells to be used.

Our medical and scientific research team has well-developed autologous adult stem cell technologies, which are currently applied in our 167 medical centers in 35 countries globally to treat many diseases. In compliance with the US FDA and the MOH regulations, the training covers the following three advanced innovative clinical technologies in regenerative medicine and investigational stem cell research and treatment methods:

Autologous Adult Stem Cell Technologies

  • Autologous adult adipose-derived SC Treatment
  • Autologous Bone-Marrow-derived SC Treatment
  • Autologous adult Platelet-Rich-Plasma isolation and processing

Allogeneic Adult Stem Cells sources

Allogeneic adult stem cells (from a donor) may be used from the following sources, under our approved procedural protocols and per applicable laws in each country:

  • Umbilical cord blood UCB
  • Placental blood and tissue
  • Wharton's Jelly Mesenchymal Stem Cells
  • Peripheral fluid (in the uterine sac)
  • Venous Blood
  • Exosomes
  • CAR-T Cells
  • NK-Cells
  • Peptides

Studies on this therapy method demonstrate a positive effect, for example, even in cases of severe osteoarthritis, wound healing disorders, scar treatment, injuries to the muscles, tendons, and ligaments while also carrying a low risk of unwanted side effects.

Allogeneic Stem Cells

Are derived from genetically different individuals and hence have the donors’ DNA and express the donors’ HLA molecules.

Autologous Adult Adipose-Derived Stem Cells (AD-MSCs)

  1. Biological source: Patient’s own fat tissue (Donor-receiver one person)
  2. Cell fitness/age effect: Cells as old as the patient; diabetes, ischemia, chemo, or aging can impair cell potency
  3. Procedure: Same-day procedure, minimal manipulation, no expansion
  4. Processing:
    • 15–20 min. mini-liposuction under local anesthesia by the doctor
    • 60–120 min. mechanical shredding or brief enzymatic isolation, incubation, in-house laboratory
  5. Deployment: 10-15 min. Immediate intra-lesional injection
  6. Cell dose: 0.5–2 × 10⁶ nucleated SVF cells / 100 mL fat
  7. Minimal manipulation: Mechanical harvest, isolation and deployment, (or very short collagenase digestion)
  8. Regulatory category: Falls under the FDA same-day tissue-tract exception and hospital exemption. Not as a licensed drug
  9. Immunology: Self → no rejection, same DNA code, no immunosuppressive drugs needed
  10. Safety profile: Autologous SVFs are safest immunologically, moderate surgical risks only (small scar, bruise, rare infection or local anesthesia complication - bad taste!)
  11. Cost:
    • High per-patient (operating room + disposables + labor, x1 patient)
    • No pharma-scale overheads, often sufficient
  12. Repeat treatments: Fresh Cells. New liposuction each cycle
  13. Clinical Efficacy (MSC):
    • Knee OA: pain ↓, MRI cartilage fill at 6 month
    • ED: IIEF score ↑ 5 points at 3 m in 30 % men
    • Cardiac, osteoarthritis, diabetic ulcers show benefit
    • Immunomodulation
  14. Clinical Efficacy (Oncology): In multiple myeloma, a 2nd auto-SCT gave superior overall & progression-free survival
  15. For hematological malignancies: The choice is disease-specific: autologous stem cell therapy dominates Myeloma
  16. Limitations: Cell number & potency fall with age/ co-morbidities. Only local or low-dose systemic use
  17. When to choose:
    • Regulations allow same-day cell and tissue use
    • Patient accepts minor surgery - Informed Consent
    • Single-joint or small-volume local lesion
    • Absolute zero immune risk
  18. Clinical-grade availability: Limited by patient’s health

MSCs CD MARKERS

Autologous Adult ADIPOSE-DERIVED MESENCHYMAL STEM CELLS (AD-MSCs)

These cells are routinely identified by the following core set of positive and negative CD antigens. The table below summarizes passages 2–4, human sub-cutaneous lipo-aspirates.

Positive (≥ 90 %) Negative (≤ 2 %)
CD29 (integrin β1) CD31 (endothelial)
CD44 (hyaluronate) CD34 (hematopoietic)*
CD73 (ecto-5′-NT) CD45 (pan-leukocyte)
CD90 (Thy-1) CD11b (myeloid)
CD105 (endoglin) CD14 (monocyte)
CD13 (aminopeptidase) CD19 (B-cell)
CD166 (ALCAM) HLA-DR (MHC-II)

*CD34 is positive in the freshly isolated stromal vascular fraction SVF and during the first 1–2 passages; expression is lost once the cells are expanded to standard clinical passages, so most laboratories still list CD34 as a negative marker for “cultured” AD-MSCs.

  • CD106 (VCAM-1) and CD271 (NGFR) are also absent on classic AD-MSCs, distinguishing them from bone-marrow MSCs.
  • HLA-ABC (MHC-I) is present at low/intermediate levels, whereas HLA-DR (MHC-II) is consistently negative, underpinning their low immunogenicity.
